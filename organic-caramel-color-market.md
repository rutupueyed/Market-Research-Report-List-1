<p><p>The organic caramel color market is divided into four classes based on production methods and applications. Class I is produced without ammonium compounds, often used in beverages. Class II involves food-grade acids and is suitable for various food products. Class III is made using sulfites, commonly used in confectionery and baked goods. Class IV is produced with acid processing and is widely used in sauces, gravies, and soups, offering deep coloring and enhancing visual appeal in diverse culinary applications.</p></p>
