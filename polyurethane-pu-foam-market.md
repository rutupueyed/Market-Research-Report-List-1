<p><p>The polyurethane (PU) foam market is primarily divided into two types: rigid foam and flexible foam. Rigid foam is characterized by its high structural integrity and is commonly used in insulation applications, construction, and refrigeration due to its energy-efficient properties. In contrast, flexible foam is known for its softness and adaptability, making it ideal for applications such as cushions, mattresses, and automotive interiors. Both types of foam play crucial roles in various industries, driven by their unique properties and applications.</p></p>
