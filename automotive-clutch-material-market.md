<p><ul><li>Friction Clutch</li><li>Centrifugal Clutch</li><li>Semi Centrifugal Clutch</li><li>Hydraulic Clutch</li><li>Vacuum Clutch</li><li>Electro-magnetic Clutch</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive clutch material market consists of several types, each designed for specific applications. Friction clutches use friction materials to engage and disengage power, while centrifugal clutches automatically engage based on engine speed. Semi centrifugal clutches combine features of both, offering variable engagement. Hydraulic clutches utilize fluid pressure for smooth operation, and vacuum clutches rely on vacuum forces for actuation. Electro-magnetic clutches leverage electromagnetic forces for engagement, providing precise control and response, making them suitable for advanced systems and applications.</p></p>
