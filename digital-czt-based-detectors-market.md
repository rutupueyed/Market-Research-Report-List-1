<p><p>Digital CZT-based detectors encompass two primary market types: direct-conversion and photoconductive types. Direct-conversion detectors convert incident X-ray photons directly into electrical signals, providing higher energy resolution and improved imaging quality. In contrast, photoconductive detectors rely on light-induced electrical conductivity changes to generate signals, typically offering faster response times. Both types are widely used in medical imaging and security applications, capitalizing on the unique properties of cadmium zinc telluride (CZT) for enhanced performance in various detection scenarios.</p></p>
