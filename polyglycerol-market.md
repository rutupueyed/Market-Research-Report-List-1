<p><p>The polyglycerol market is segmented into various types based on the number of glycerol units. PG2 (diglycerol) has two glycerol units, ideal for emollients and food applications. PG3 (triglycerol) contains three units, often used in cosmetics and pharmaceuticals. PG4 (tetraglycerol) contains four units and finds use in food emulsifiers. PG6 and PG10, with six and ten units respectively, are utilized in specialized industrial applications. The "Other" segment encompasses diverse formulations catering to niche markets.</p></p>
