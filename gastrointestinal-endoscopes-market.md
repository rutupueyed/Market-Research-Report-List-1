<p><p>The gastrointestinal endoscopes market is primarily classified into two types: flexible and rigid endoscopes. Flexible endoscopes are designed to navigate through curved anatomical structures, allowing for minimally invasive procedures. They are used for diagnostic and therapeutic interventions in areas like the esophagus, stomach, and intestines. Rigid endoscopes, on the other hand, maintain a fixed shape and are typically employed in procedures requiring direct visualization of specific areas. Both types serve essential roles in gastroenterology, enhancing patient care and treatment outcomes.</p></p>
