<p><p>The traditional thermal cycler market comprises several types, notably real-time thermal cyclers and gradient thermal cyclers. Real-time thermal cyclers enable the monitoring of DNA amplification as it occurs, providing instant data and facilitating quantitative PCR. In contrast, gradient thermal cyclers allow for simultaneous testing of multiple temperatures within a single run, optimizing conditions for various primer sets. Both types enhance PCR efficiency, cater to diverse research needs, and are essential tools in molecular biology and genetic research.</p></p>
