<p><p>The dehydrated seafood market includes several drying techniques. Spray drying involves quickly evaporating moisture, resulting in fine powders ideal for instant products. Freeze drying preserves texture and flavor by sublimating ice from frozen seafood. Vacuum drying removes moisture under reduced pressure, enhancing shelf life. Sun drying uses natural sunlight, providing a traditional method, while hot air drying employs heated air for effective moisture removal. Other methods may include microwave or osmotic drying, each offering unique benefits and applications in food preservation.</p></p>
