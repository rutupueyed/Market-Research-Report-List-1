<p><p>Clinical Research Organizations (CROs) play a crucial role in conducting clinical trials across four major phases. Phase I trials assess safety and dosage in a small group of healthy participants. Phase II trials evaluate efficacy and side effects in a larger patient group. Phase III trials confirm effectiveness, monitor adverse reactions, and compare with standard treatments in diverse populations. Phase IV trials, conducted post-approval, gather long-term safety data and monitor drug performance in the general population, ensuring ongoing evaluation of therapeutic benefits.</p></p>
